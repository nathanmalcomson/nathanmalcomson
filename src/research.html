Research Interests

My dissertation pursues an act-based account of meaning, along the lines of Scott Soames' What is Meaning? and Rethinking Language, Mind, and Meaning as well as Peter Hanks' Propositional Content. However, I depart from both Soames and Hanks at important junctures. An act-based account of meaning, as I understand it, is a position in meta-semantics: it's an attempt to articulate the facts by virtue of which we mean what we do by our words. While one might justifiably think of act-based accounts as a species of use theories of meaning, I think there are distinct explanatory advantages to making a robust and explicit appeal to the nature and shape of intentional action in an account of meaning.

I have other interests in the philosophy of language as well. Most notably, I am working on motivating an anti-conventional literalism which I think one finds in the work of Davidson. That is is to say, I am interested in arguing against various forms of contextualism and against social conventionalism.

Outside of the philosophy of language, I have interests in the philosophy of action and in the philosophy of religion, as well as an abiding interest in the works and influence of both Wittgenstein and Anscombe.
