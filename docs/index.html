Bio

I completed a BA in English at the University of British Columbia. My honour's thesis was on contemporary readings of Shakespeare's much-maligned Titus Andronicus. Thinking about hermeneutics led me to develop an interest in the philosophy of language, specifically in what makes it the case that we mean what we do by our words. So, I did an MA in Philosophy at York University, Toronto, and am now pursuing my PhD at York. With two young boys at home, most of my non-philosophy time is taken up with trucks, legos, and conflict resolution. In those rare moments of free time, I enjoy hiking, boardgames, and reading fiction.
